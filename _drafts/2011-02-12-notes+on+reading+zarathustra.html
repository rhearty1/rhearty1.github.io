"I dare to propose a new way to unravel this thematic tangle. This is to focus on the work's existential overtone [featured predominantly in the songs: The Seven Seals, The Other Dancing Song, The Convalescent, On the Great Longing, etc.] and read it as a poem that features Zarathustra as a tragic hero" not in the sense that the work should be regarded as a tragedy, but (like Goethe's Faust) as an epic poem.
