The concept of a "nation" is an artificial construct which arose in a piecemeal fashion out of the need of a quasi-geographic/cultural populace to defend itself in a way that is characteristically 'human': the (at first, ad-hoc) creation of a "border" with weapons pointing out, presuming that the worst antagonism would arise from the outlying lands (in the form of raids). I say human because this is a construction wrapped up with our consciousness of the world as a dichotomy between internal and external.
